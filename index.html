Gender, Equality, and Sexuality in Scandinavia

This summer, I took a 3 week long course on gender, equality, and sexuality in Scandinavia. I learned a lot of interesting things about how Scandinavia is great, but also how it still has a lot more to improve. The following is what I've learned through pictoral form.

Parental Leave in Sweden vs. USA


In Sweden, parents are granted 480 days of parental leave for each child that they have. They can use these days as they wish up until their child is 8 years old. The father is required to take a minimum of 60 days. In the USA, there is no legally mandated minimum parental leave.

Trans People and Sterilization


I was horrified to learn that from 1972 up until 2013, Sweden required that trans people become sterilized before they could attain legal gender recognition. People who were sterilized during this time were later provided compensation of roughly $25,000, but even that seems like not enough when discussing removing someone's ability to procreate. Sweden was also the first country to pay damages to trans victims of forced sterilization. Hopefully fewer countries will have this requirement and those that do will also abolish this requirement.

Germanic vs. Nordic System for Sex Work


I didn't really have any previous knowledge about prostitution or that its regulation varied in different parts of the world. I was surprised to found out that Germany had a very well established and protective position on sex work, especially in contrast to Scandinavian countries. In the Nordic model, sex work is decriminalized, but in the Germanic model, it is legalized. The difference between decriminalization and legalization is that decriminalization means that the government removes any laws against sex work, but legalization means that there is an active system of governmental regulation so that sex workers are licensed and required to work in specific ways.

Feminist Porn


I had never really heard of feminist porn prior to taking this class. I was aware of the masculine, voyuer perspective that most porn strives for, along with its problematic effects on men's interaction with women (namely they become more violent). Feminist porn is focused more on creating an ethical environment for sex workers and porn with a storyline.
